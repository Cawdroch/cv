Chris Cawdron

Aviation Engineer specialising in early Gas Turbine Engines

I was born and educated in the Midlands and completed a craft apprenticeship with the Ministry of Defence at the Royal Aircraft Establishment Bedford as well as attaining a BTEC HNC in Aerospace Engineering. I was trained in both airframe and engine disciplines but started to specialise in engines early on in my career. I moved onto to work full time at Cosworth Engineering in Northampton whilst maintaining my aviation experience as a part time volunteer with Classic Aviation Projects. At Cosworth, I was initially building race and high performance road car engines and then went on to work as technical and design lead on engine assembly procedures, tooling and rigs. I then moved onto the design of testbed installations and associated test rigs, this all culminating in project managing the installation of brand new 'state of the art' test bed installations for both IC engines and electric motors with responsibility towards budget and timescale constraints. I left Cosworth (by then Mahle Powertrain) in 2017 to re-engage with my original career as an Aircraft Engineer. I now support the vintage aviation community both in the UK and abroad and I specialise in defining and performing CAA compliant Calendar Life Extension Programs on first and second generation Gas Turbine Engines.
